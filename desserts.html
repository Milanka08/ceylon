Desserts 

 Walithalapa

 Ingredients:

 2 cups rice flour 

1 cup grated jaggery (or brown sugar) 

1 cup thick coconut milk 

1/4 teaspoon ground cardamom 

A pinch of salt 

Banana leaves or aluminum foil for wrapping 

Instructions: 

In a mixing bowl, combine the rice flour, grated jaggery, coconut milk, ground cardamom, and salt. Mix well until all the ingredients are thoroughly combined and the batter is smooth. Take a large piece of banana leaf or aluminum foil and lightly grease it with oil to prevent sticking. Place a small portion of the batter onto the leaf and spread it out to form a thin layer, about 1/4 inch thick. Fold the leaf or foil over the batter to create a neat parcel. Repeat this process with the remaining batter. Steam the parcels in a steamer for about 30-40 minutes or until the Thalapa is cooked and firm. Once cooked, remove the parcels from the steamer and allow them to cool slightly. Unwrap the parcels and cut the Thalapa into desired shapes, such as squares or diamonds. Serve the Thalapa warm or at room temperature. It can be enjoyed on its own as a sweet snack or served with a cup of tea. Note: If banana leaves are not available, you can use aluminum foil to wrap the Thalapa. Simply cut the foil into squares, place the batter on each square, and fold it over to form a parcel. Enjoy your homemade Sri Lankan Thalapa!

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

   

 

 

Pani walalu 

 Ingredients:

 1 cup urad dal (black gram) 

1 cup grated jaggery (or brown sugar) 

1/2 cup grated coconut 

1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder

 Oil for deep frying 

Instructions: 

Rinse the urad dal thoroughly under running water. Soak it in water for 3-4 hours or overnight. Drain the soaked dal. Grind the drained dal in a food processor or blender until it becomes a smooth paste. Add a small amount of water if needed to achieve a thick batter consistency. In a mixing bowl, combine the ground urad dal paste, grated jaggery, grated coconut, and cardamom powder. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated. Heat oil in a deep pan or kadai over medium heat for frying. Take a small portion of the batter and shape it into a small ball using your hands. Flatten it slightly to form a small disc. Repeat this process with the remaining batter. Gently drop a few prepared discs into the hot oil and fry them until they turn golden brown and crispy. Make sure to fry them in batches, not overcrowding the pan. Once fried, remove the Pani Walalu from the oil using a slotted spoon and drain the excess oil on a paper towel. Allow the Pani Walalu to cool completely before serving. They can be stored in an airtight container for a few days. Enjoy the crispy and sweet Pani Walalu as a delightful treat with a cup of tea or coffee! Note: It's important to maintain the oil temperature while frying. If the oil is too hot, the outer layer may get dark quickly while the inside remains uncooked. If the oil is not hot enough, the Pani Walalu may absorb too much oil and become greasy. Adjust the heat accordingly while frying.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Lavariya 

   Ingredients:

 2 cups rice flour 

1 cup freshly grated coconut 

1 cup jaggery (or brown sugar) 

1/4 teaspoon ground cardamom 

A pinch of salt 

Water (as needed) 

Instructions: 

In a large bowl, combine the rice flour, grated coconut, jaggery, ground cardamom, and a pinch of salt. Mix them well. Slowly add water, a little at a time, and knead the mixture to form a soft dough. The consistency should be similar to a chapati dough. Take small portions of the dough and shape them into small cylinders or oval-shaped dumplings, about 2-3 inches long. Repeat with the remaining dough. Steam the dumplings in a steamer or an idli maker for about 15-20 minutes, until they are cooked through and firm. You can insert a toothpick or knife into the dumplings to check if they are cooked. Once cooked, remove the dumplings from the steamer and let them cool slightly. Serve the Lavariya warm or at room temperature. They can be enjoyed as is or with a cup of tea. Note: Some variations of Lavariya include adding roasted sesame seeds or cashews to the dough for added texture and flavor. I hope you enjoy making and savoring this traditional Sri Lankan sweet dish!
