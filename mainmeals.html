Fried Rice 

 Ingredients:

 2 cups cooked rice (preferably cooled and day-old rice) 

2 tablespoons vegetable oil 

1 onion, finely chopped 

2 cloves of garlic, minced 

1-inch piece of ginger, grated 

1 carrot, diced 

1 bell pepper, diced 

1 cup cooked chicken, shrimp, or any other protein of your choice (optional) 

2 tablespoons soy sauce 

1 tablespoon curry powder 

1 teaspoon chili flakes (adjust to taste) 

Salt, to taste 

Freshly ground black pepper, to taste 

2 green onions, sliced (for garnish) 

Fresh cilantro leaves, chopped (for garnish)

 Lime wedges (for serving) 

Instructions:

 Heat the vegetable oil in a large pan or wok over medium-high heat. Add the chopped onion, minced garlic, and grated ginger to the pan. Sauté for 2-3 minutes until the onions are translucent and aromatic. Add the diced carrot and bell pepper to the pan. Stir-fry for about 3-4 minutes until the vegetables are slightly tender. If you're using cooked chicken, shrimp, or any other protein, add it to the pan and stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes until heated through. Push the vegetables and protein to one side of the pan and add the cooked rice to the other side. Break up any clumps of rice with a spatula or fork. Drizzle the soy sauce over the rice and sprinkle the curry powder and chili flakes on top. Mix everything together, ensuring that the rice is evenly coated with the sauce and spices. Season with salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste. Continue to stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes until the rice is heated through and well combined with the vegetables and protein. Remove the pan from the heat. Garnish the fried rice with sliced green onions and chopped cilantro. Serve the Sri Lankan fried rice hot, accompanied by lime wedges for squeezing over the rice. Enjoy your homemade Sri Lankan fried rice!

Pol Sambola 

Ingredients: 

2 cups freshly grated coconut (or desiccated coconut) 

4-5 red or green chili peppers (adjust according to your spice preference) 

2-3 cloves of garlic 

1 small onion, chopped 

1 tablespoon lime juice (or tamarind pulp) 

Salt, to taste 

Instructions: 

In a food processor or blender, combine the grated coconut, chili peppers, garlic, and chopped onion. Blend the mixture until it becomes a coarse paste. You can adjust the texture according to your preference—some prefer a smoother consistency while others prefer it slightly chunky. Transfer the mixture to a bowl and add the lime juice (or tamarind pulp) and salt. Mix everything together thoroughly, ensuring that the lime juice and salt are evenly incorporated. Taste the Pol Sambola and adjust the seasoning if needed by adding more salt or lime juice according to your taste. Once the flavors are well combined, cover the bowl and let the Pol Sambola sit for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together. After the resting period, give the Pol Sambola a final mix and it's ready to be served. Pol Sambola can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to a week. It is traditionally served with rice and curry, hoppers, string hoppers, or bread. Enjoy the spicy and flavorful Sri Lankan Pol Sambola!  

 

Cashew curry 

 1 cup cashew nuts 

2 tablespoons vegetable oil 

1 onion, finely chopped 

2 cloves of garlic, minced 

1-inch piece of ginger, grated 

2 green chilies, sliced (adjust to taste) 

1 teaspoon curry powder 

1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder 

1 teaspoon chili powder (adjust to taste) 

1 cup coconut milk 

Salt, to taste 

 

Fresh cilantro leaves, chopped (for garnish) 

Instructions: 

Soak the cashew nuts in water for about 2 hours. Drain and set aside. Heat the vegetable oil in a pan or skillet over medium heat. Add the chopped onion to the pan and sauté until it becomes translucent. Add the minced garlic, grated ginger, and sliced green chilies to the pan. Sauté for another minute until fragrant. Add the curry powder, turmeric powder, and chili powder to the pan. Stir well to coat the onions and spices evenly. Add the soaked cashew nuts to the pan and stir to combine with the onion and spice mixture. Pour in the coconut milk and season with salt to taste. Stir everything together. Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer and reduce the heat to low. Cover the pan and let it cook for about 15-20 minutes, or until the cashews are tender and the flavors have melded together. Once the cashews are cooked and the curry has thickened slightly, remove the pan from the heat. Garnish the cashew curry with freshly chopped cilantro leaves. Serve the cashew curry hot with steamed rice or naan bread. Enjoy the delicious and creamy Cashew Curry!
